# English/CA Resume Conventions

## Personal Information Rules
- NO photo
- NO date of birth
- NO marital status
- NO religion, social insurance number (SIN), or health status
- NO full mailing address — city + province only (e.g., "Gatineau, QC", "Ottawa,ON", "Moncton, NB", "Fredericton, NB")
- INCLUDE: full name, phone number (with area code), professional email. LinkedIn URL and Github URL are optional
- LinkedIn URL: ensure profile matches resume content
- Github URL: ensure profile matches resume content

## Section Order
1. Contact Information (name, phone, email. LinkedIn and Github are optional)
2. Profil Summary (bullet list)
3. Technical Skills / Technical Competencies (grouped by functional category)
4. Work Experience (reverse chronological)
5. Education
6. Certifications / Accreditations (if applicable)

## Length
- < 10 years experience: 2 pages (2 pages is highly accepted in Canada)
- 10+ years / senior / executive: 2 pages maximum
- Estimated ~800 words per page with standard formatting

## Profil Summary
- 4 to 8 bullets summarizing the profile, tailored to the target job posting. Style: normal 11pt text, no bold.
- Must mention: targeted Canadian role alignment, primary technical or business domain.

## Experience Section
- Reverse chronological (most recent first)
- Recent 2-4 roles: full detail
  - Company name + City, Province Company
  - Role title + dates (YYYY - YYYY or "Present" or "..." for ongoing)
  - 1-2 sentence responsibilities overview
  - 3-5 achievement bullets with metrics
- Older roles (older than 10 years): condensed
  - title and date on the same line and company next line
  - 2-8 most relevant achievements only[cite: 1]

## Achievement Bullet Formula
```
[Strong Action Verb] [specific task/system] [scope/context], [resulting in / achieving] [quantified outcome]
```

### Approved Action Verbs (Canadian Professional Context)
Led, Architected, Engineered, Delivered, Streamlined, Modernized, Implemented, Scaled, Automated, Reduced, Increased, Collaborated, Orchestrated, Fabricated, Managed, Built, Created, Identified, Designed, Reduced, Developed, Applied, Updated, Documented

### Avoid
Responsible for, Tasked with, Assisted with, Worked on, Participated in, Was involved in

## Skills Section
- Group by functional category (3-6 categories, matching terms in the Job Description)
- Each category: 4-6 skills, pipe-delimited or comma-separated
- Use Canadian industry standard terminology (e.g., spelling variations like *Programmes* vs *Programs* depending on the language track, though standard English/US spelling is widely accepted in tech, maintain consistency)
- Example:
 ```
Software Engineering: .NET Core | C# | ASP.NET | C++ | Clean Architecture
Front-End Development: React | React Router | HTML5 / CSS3 | JavaScript | TypeScript
Version Control Systems: Git, TortoiseGit
AI Developer Tools & Agents: Claude Code, OpenCode, OpenAI Codex, GitHub Copilot.
  ```

## Education
- Positioned after the experience section (unless a recent graduate within the last 1-2 years)
- Credential earned (e.g., Bachelor of Science, Diploma of College Studies / DEC), institution, province/location, graduation year
- No GPA unless specifically requested by a public sector or academic institution
- Relevant coursework: only if directly applicable and < 3 years experience
- Do not include high school education if post-secondary education is completed

## Tone & Language
- Use Canadian English spelling conventions consistently if required by the employer (e.g., *optimise* vs *optimize*, *behaviour* vs *behavior*), though standard tech resumes generally accept standard North American English as long as it is consistent throughout.
- Active voice throughout
- Direct and definitive language ("Engineered" not "Assisted in engineering", "Delivered" not "Helped deliver")
- Quantify accomplishments wherever possible (e.g., budgets managed, percentages optimized, processing times reduced)
- No first-person pronouns ("I", "my") — implied by context[cite: 1]
- Professional, clear, and ATS-friendly formatting (avoid complex multi-column graphics or text boxes)
